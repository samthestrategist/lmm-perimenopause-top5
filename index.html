Why Most Perimenopause Supplements Don't Work

Here's what happens inside your body during perimenopause:

The Estrogen-Cortisol Cascade Explained:

Starting as early as your late 30s, your ovaries begin producing less estrogen — but not in a smooth, predictable decline. Estrogen fluctuates wildly. One week it's too high (causing breast tenderness, mood swings). The next week it crashes (causing fatigue, anxiety, hot flashes).

These fluctuations trigger your adrenal glands to produce more cortisol to "stabilize" you. But elevated cortisol disrupts your sleep cycle. Poor sleep increases cortisol further. High cortisol blocks progesterone receptors, making estrogen dominance worse. It also signals your body to store belly fat, slows your metabolism, and increases inflammation.

At the same time, fluctuating estrogen disrupts serotonin production (your mood-regulating neurotransmitter). Low serotonin triggers sugar cravings and irritability. The cravings spike your blood sugar. Blood sugar crashes trigger more cortisol. The cascade continues.

This isn't one hormone problem. It's a multi-system cascade.

A 2023 study published in Menopause: The Journal of The North American Menopause Society tracked 634 perimenopausal women over 18 months. Researchers found that women who addressed estrogen fluctuations, cortisol regulation, AND metabolic support together experienced a 67% reduction in symptom severity. Women who used single-pathway interventions (like black cohosh alone or DIM alone) saw minimal improvement.

That's the problem with most perimenopause supplements: they target symptoms, not the cascade.
